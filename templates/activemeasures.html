ESG Compliant Measures

We, at Simadri Surya Eye Hospital, make every effort to reduce wastage and thus reduce the burden on earth. In this regard, we have invested in a good Sterilization System, consisting of a 250 litres Autoclave and a Flash Sterilizer – the primary idea being to minimize the use of disposable instruments and linen for surgeries. Instead, we use instruments that can be sterilized in the sterilizer and can be reused a number of times. Apart from that, we use various linen like doctor gowns, patient gowns, drapes, towels, etc. - all of these are also not disposable ones and are sterilized.

This helps in two ways: one, it ensures that all items are adequately sterile (as eye care needs a more sterile environment than many other surgeries), and two, it is not heavy on the earth. Thus, for every surgery, we are saving a minimum of 2 kgs of material from being disposed of.

Sterilization

The above needs that we take utmost care in sterilization. The procedure followed is detailed below:

Equipment Standards

A Class B sterilizer with vacuum and steam pressure cycles, preferably with a 70-liter capacity, is ideal for centers performing 15-20 surgeries daily.

Annual checks by a service engineer are mandatory for equipment functionality.

The steam sterilizer must maintain:

  • Temperature: 121∘C for 12-15 minutes or 134∘C for 8-12 minutes.
  • Pressure: 2.2-2.5 Pascals.
  • Cycle Time: Approximately 40-45 minutes per cycle.

Vacuum Cycle

  • Pulse Cycle: Three pulses test the solenoid valve for pressure release and vacuum creation.
  • Heating Mode: Slow heating until the chamber reaches the set temperature.
  • Sterilization Mode: Maintaining the temperature at 121∘C for the set duration under 2.2-2.4 Pascals pressure.
  • Drying Mode: After sterilization, the chamber dries, reducing pressure to zero.

Confirmation of Successful Sterilization

  • Monitor temperature, pressure, and duration.
  • Perform daily Bowie Dick tests, changing from yellow to purple to indicate success.
  • Use biological indicators fortnightly to ensure bacteria elimination.
  • TST indicators with each load change color to confirm sterilization; any deviation requires re-sterilization.
  • Tape indicators on items turn black post-sterilization and are recorded for tracking.
  • Items remain sterile for 48 hours; unused items are re-sterilized.

Flash Sterilizer

A flash sterilizer, with shorter cycles, is used for smaller items like probes and sleeves, following similar principles to ensure safety and sterility in ophthalmic surgeries.
