About us

Simadri Surya Eye Hospital stands as a beacon of hope in Nabarangpur, Odisha, providing essential eye care services to the underserved tribal populations and those below the poverty line. It embodies the Simadri Misra and Susheela Public Charitable Trustsâ€™ commitment to restoring vision for those in need. Addressing the lack of comprehensive eye care in the region, the hospital fills a critical gap by offering secondary-level care, preventing avoidable blindness due to cataracts and treating conditions like Glaucoma, Orbit & Oculoplasty and Diabetic Retinopathy. With a strong commitment to serve, the hospital ensures 80% of its treatments are free for the poor, while others receive care at subsidized rates. This initiative not only brings specialized care closer to home but also raises awareness and accessibility, lighting the path to a brighter future for many.

Susheela Public Charitable Trust

The "Susheela Public Charitable Trust", renowned for its successful management of eye hospitals in rural and tribal areas, epitomizes a dedicated effort towards improving eye health care in underserved communities. With a focus on providing comprehensive eye care services, the Trust leverages its expertise in hospital management to ensure that individuals in remote locations have access to quality eye treatments and surgeries. This initiative not only addresses the critical need for specialized health services in these regions but also contributes significantly to reducing the prevalence of preventable blindness among the most vulnerable populations, embodying a commitment to enhancing the quality of life through improved vision care.

  • Though a New Trust, people behind it have more than 20 years' experience in eye care and management of hospitals at a very senior level.
  • Have experience of conducting over 7,000 eye camps and more than 4 lakh eye surgeries, particularly in Tribal, Rural areas.
  • Have a strong presence in National and International Eye Care Associations.
  • Have experience of conducting more than 60 eye camps in remote Tribal areas of Odisha and Chattisgarh, and conducting more than 3,000 surgeries in this area.

    • Simadri Misra Charitable Trust

    The "Simadri Misra Charitable Trust," established in 2002 in memory of the freedom fighter and advocate Sri Simadri Misra, continues his legacy of supporting tribal communities in Odisha. Misra, who dedicated his life to the rights of tribals in Nabarangpur and edited the "Dandakaranya Times" to highlight their issues, is commemorated through the trust's efforts. Founded by his son, Sh. J P Misra, and currently managed by family members, the trust offers free education, boarding, and other essentials to 150 tribal students from BPL families across Odisha in a hostel in Bhubaneswar. The trust, recognized for its contributions with Section 80G and CSR registrations, exemplifies sustained commitment to tribal upliftment.

  • Have a strong presence in Odisha & Chattisgarh.
  • Currently Training about 200 children by providing free boarding & Lodging at Bhubaneswar.
  • Constructed 16,000 sft Hospital building & 3,000 sft Guest house without taking any external support
  • Quality is not compromised at any level.
  • Children participated and won medals at National Level.
