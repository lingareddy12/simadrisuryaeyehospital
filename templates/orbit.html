What is Orbit Oculoplasty Surgery?

Orbit oculoplasty disorders refer to a range of medical conditions and diseases affecting the orbit (the bony cavity containing the eye), the eyelids, and the surrounding structures. Oculoplastic surgery, also known as ophthalmic plastic and reconstructive surgery, is a specialized branch of ophthalmology that deals with the diagnosis and surgical treatment of these disorders

Who are at Risk .. ?

  • People over 60
  • People having Autoimmune Disorders
  • People having Thyroid Disease
  • people having Eye/Facial injuries
  • People with diabetes

    • Symptoms

  • Drooping of the upper eyelid
  • Painless lump or swelling on the eyelid.
  • Pain, swelling
  • Excessive tearing
  • Bulging eyes.
  • nward turning of the eyelid.

    • Major types of Orbit & Oculoplasty Disorders

    Eyelid Disorders

  • Ptosis: Drooping of the upper eyelid, which can affect vision.
  • Entropion: Inward turning of the eyelid, causing irritation as eyelashes rub against the eye.
  • Ectropion: Outward turning of the eyelid, leading to exposure and drying of the eye.
  • Blepharitis: Inflammation of the eyelids, often with redness, swelling, and crusting.
  • Eyelid Tumors: Benign or malignant growths on the eyelids.

    • Orbital Disorders

  • Orbital Fractures:Breaks in the bones surrounding the eye, often due to trauma.
  • Orbital Tumors:Growths in the orbit, which can be benign (e.g., hemangioma) or malignant (e.g., orbital lymphoma).
  • Thyroid Eye Disease (Graves' Ophthalmopathy):An autoimmune condition causing inflammation and swelling in the orbit.
  • Orbital Cellulitis:A serious infection of the tissues around the eye, causing pain, redness, and swelling.
