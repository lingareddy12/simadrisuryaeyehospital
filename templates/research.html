MOMERANDUM OF UNDERSTANDING

Medical imaging encompasses different imaging modalities and processes to image the human body for diagnostic and treatment purposes to improve public health. On this ground "Simadri Surya Eye Hospital" is committed to work with utmost diligence and dedication with "Center for Medical Imaging Studies - Gayatri Vidya Parishad College of Engineering (Autonomous), Visakhapatnam", for development and implementation of AI-based algorithms that enabling the doctors and paramedical staff for accurate detection and early diagnosis of patient’s complications related to ophthalmic image analysis.

CENTER FOR MEDICAL IMAGING STUDIES

The Center for Medical Imaging Studies (CMIS) is a distinguished research center situated within the Electronics and Communication Engineering (ECE) department at Gayatri Vidya Parishad College of Engineering (A), located in Visakhapatnam. Established in 2019 under the leadership of Dr. Birendra Biswal, CMIS has emerged as a hub for cutting-edge research in the field of medical image processing, leveraging the power of Artificial Intelligence (AI) to drive innovative solutions and advancements. Through the integration of AI techniques with medical imaging technologies, CMIS aims to enhance diagnostic accuracy, streamline medical workflows, and ultimately improve patient care outcomes.Under the guidance of Dr. Biswal,Ms. Geetha Pavani, senior Research Associate at Simadri Surya Eye Hospital, monitors a team of B.Tech students working in the area of medical image processing to detect retinal disorders at an early stage.

A robust segmentation of retinal fluids from OCT images using MCFAR-net

This paper presents a novel architecture to detect Macular Edema (ME) in Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT) images that occurs due to high fluid accumulation between the retinal layers of the eye. These excessive fluids have swollen the macular region and may result in visual impairments. To alleviate this problem in its early stage, the proposed model, Multiscale Context Enhancive Aggregation & Refinement Network (MCFAR-Net) is implemented to detect these fluids enabling the prevention of visual loss.
