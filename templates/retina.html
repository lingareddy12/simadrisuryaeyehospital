What is Medical Retina?

Medical retina is a subspecialty within ophthalmology that focuses on the diagnosis and treatment of diseases and conditions affecting the retina, the light-sensitive layer at the back of the eye. The retina plays a crucial role in vision by converting light into neural signals, which are then processed by the brain to create visual images. Disorders of the retina can significantly impact vision and overall eye health.

Who are at Risk .. ?

  • People over 60
  • Family History
  • People with Obesity
  • People with Diabetes
  • People with Hypertension

    • Symptoms

  • Blurry Vision
  • Dark Areas of Vision
  • Sudden Vision Loss
  • Sudden Increase in Floaters
  • Night Blindness
  • Straight Lines Appear Bent

    • Major types of Medical Retina

    Age-Related Macular Degeneration (AMD)

  • Dry AMD:Characterized by the thinning of the macula and the presence of drusen (yellow deposits). It progresses slowly and leads to gradual vision loss.
  • Wet AMD:Caused by abnormal blood vessels growing under the retina and leaking fluid or blood, leading to rapid and severe vision loss.

    • Diabetic Retinopathy

  • Non-proliferative DR:Early stage, characterized by microaneurysms, hemorrhages, and retinal swelling.
  • Proliferative DR:Advanced stage, involving the growth of new, abnormal blood vessels on the retina, which can bleed and cause scar tissue.

    • Retinal Detachment

  • Rhegmatogenous Retinal Detachment: Caused by a tear or break in the retina that allows fluid to get under it and separate it from the underlying tissue.
  • Tractional Retinal Detachment:Occurs when scar tissue on the retina’s surface contracts and pulls the retina away from the back of the eye
  • Exudative Retinal Detachment:Caused by fluid leaking under the retina without any tears or breaks.

    • Macular Edema

  • Retinitis Pigmentosa (RP):A group of genetic disorders causing retinal degeneration and progressive vision loss, often starting with night blindness.
  • Stargardt Disease:A genetic disorder leading to macular degeneration in young people, causing central vision loss.
